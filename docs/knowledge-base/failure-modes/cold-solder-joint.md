# Cold Solder Joint

## Summary

A cold joint forms when the solder does not fully reflow and wet the surfaces, leaving a
dull, grainy, high-resistance connection. It may pass at room temperature yet fail
intermittently under load or thermal cycling.

## Symptoms

- Intermittent continuity that changes with flex or temperature.
- Marginal resistance readings that drift between retests.
- Dull, grainy, or cracked joint appearance.

## Likely causes

- Insufficient soldering temperature or dwell time.
- Joint disturbed before the solder solidified.
- Contaminated or oxidized pad or lead surface.

## ICT signature

A cold joint often reads a small but non-zero series resistance instead of a clean near-
zero ohm path. Readings that vary across repeated flying-probe touches on the same joint
are a strong indicator. Borderline values that hover near the test limit warrant a manual
re-probe.

## Corrective actions

- Re-flow the joint with adequate heat and fresh flux.
- Clean oxidized surfaces before reworking.
- Re-measure series resistance to confirm a clean joint.
